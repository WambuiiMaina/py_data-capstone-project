# Cervical Cancer Screening Analysis

##  Problem
Cervical cancer remains a major public health challenge, particularly in low- and middle-income countries like Kenya. Despite being preventable through early screening, uptake remains low, leaving many women at risk of late diagnosis and poor outcomes. There is a need to better understand which populations are being reached—and which are being left behind.

---

## Solution
This project analyzes cervical cancer screening patterns using demographic and socioeconomic data. The goal is to identify key factors influencing screening uptake and highlight gaps in access, awareness, and utilization of preventive services.

---

## Tools Used
- Python  
- Pandas  
- Matplotlib  
---

## Key Insights
- Screening uptake is significantly low among the population studied  
- Older women are more likely to be screened compared to younger women  
- Higher levels of education are associated with increased screening rates  
- Women in urban areas have higher screening uptake than those in rural areas  
- Socioeconomic status plays a major role in access to screening services  

---

## Recommendations
- Expand mobile and community-based screening programs to reach underserved areas  
- Increase awareness through media and community outreach initiatives  
- Improve accessibility of screening services in rural regions  
- Leverage healthcare workers and local networks to promote early screening
